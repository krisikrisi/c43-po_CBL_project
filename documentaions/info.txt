In here we have the final report which documents a digitally twinned autonomous TurtleBot system by detailing its full development lifecycle, performance evaluation, and team reflection.
It consists of credentials and lab access details, an Introduction that establishes the overarching Technical Solution—using revised concept maps, Context/Level 1 DFD diagrams and SDG alignments to address key stakeholder challenges—and outlines the initial Proof of Concept (PoC) scope and feasibility goals. 
The Implementation & Demonstration section presents the functional and non-functional requirements delivered, the verified digital twin characteristics and the actual executed timeline and resource usage.
In the Post-Project Evaluation section, the team analyzes differences between initial plans and actual outcomes across core technical criteria—specifically Bidirectional Communication, State Synchronization and Environmental & Object Interaction—while reviewing resource shifts, timeline variances and risk management performance. 
Finally, a 1.5-page Detailed Reflection evaluates team dynamics and project activities against the Phase 1 and Phase 5 Team Dashboards to extract future lessons, with comprehensive risk assessment tables, team dashboards and large diagrams attached in the Appendices.
